Agust Siregar

I'm a PhD candidate in Chemical Biology at Velema Lab, Radboud University, the Netherlands. My PhD is funded by the TargetRNA Marie Skłodowska-Curie Action (MSCA) network, where I focus on method development for transcriptome-wide RNA–small molecule interaction profiling.

My work sits at the interface of RNA biology, chemical biology, and bioinformatics. I develop and apply DMS-MaPseq — a chemical probing approach that reads RNA secondary structure at single-nucleotide resolution across the transcriptome — to study how small molecules selectively engage RNA targets in living cells. A key model system in my research is the Bacillus subtilis FMN riboswitch, a structured RNA element that controls gene expression in response to flavin mononucleotide, and a compelling target for antibacterial compound development.

More broadly, I am interested in how RNA structure encodes function, and how we can exploit this to discover and validate small-molecule–RNA interactions at a transcriptome-wide scale — with applications in antibiotic discovery and RNA-targeted therapeutics.

Before starting my PhD, I trained in microbiology and molecular biology, gaining experience in bacterial genetics, gene regulation, and antimicrobial compound mechanisms.

Research

My research centres on three interconnected areas:

  • RNA structure probing (DMS-MaPseq). I develop and apply chemical probing methods to map RNA structure in living bacterial cells, generating transcriptome-wide datasets that reveal how RNA folds in its native cellular environment.
  • Riboswitch biology. I study riboswitches — particularly the B. subtilis FMN riboswitch — as model systems for structure-function relationships in regulatory RNAs, and as targets for antibiotic development.
  • Transcriptome-wide RNA–small molecule interactions. I am building experimental and computational pipelines to profile, at scale, which RNAs in the transcriptome are engaged by small molecules — enabling unbiased discovery of RNA targets and off-target effects.

On the computational side, I develop custom bioinformatics pipelines in R and Python for processing DMS-MaPseq data, including mutation rate quantification, differential structure analysis, and binding-site detection using sliding-window z-score approaches.
